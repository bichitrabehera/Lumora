Design Guidelines v1

LoveyPage
Build it right, build it simple.

A complete reference for the developer — what to keep, what to cut, what to write, and exactly how the visual language should work.

01 — Philosophy

One feeling. Instant clarity.

LoveyPage should feel like a thoughtful gift — not a SaaS dashboard. Every design decision should make the visitor feel something warm immediately. If a section doesn't do that, it doesn't belong.

✓ Do
  • Generous white space — let it breathe
  • One clear action per screen
  • Warm, human-sounding copy
  • Fast decisions for the visitor
  • Serif headlines + sans-serif body
✗ Don't
  • Multiple CTAs competing for attention
  • Badge overload (trust signals everywhere)
  • Corporate jargon or marketing speak
  • Checklist-heavy hero sections
  • Dark sections mixed with pink for no reason

Cards and section backgrounds should use soft transparency over the cream base.

04 — Hero Section

Simple. One thought. One action.

The hero is where you either win or lose the visitor. Cut everything that isn't essential. Remove the checklist bullets and the badge row — these feel like landing page noise. Let the headline and one subline do the work.

♥ A new way to celebrate
The page they'll
never forget.
Beautiful, personalised pages for birthdays, anniversaries, and the moments that matter. Pick a design, fill in your story, share the link.
What to cut from the current hero
Remove The 4-item checklist (✓ Beautifully designed, ✓ Add photos…). Redundant — these are said in the subline and "How it works".
Remove The 4 trust badges (🛡️ Ad-free, 📱 Live QR, ✨ No coding, ⚡ 30 seconds). These reduce trust at this stage — explain features in "How it works" instead.
Simplify The rotating text words. Keep 2–3 max (Birthdays. Anniversaries. Proposals.) — too many dilutes meaning.
Keep The eyebrow line, main headline, one subline, and two buttons: primary CTA + ghost "See templates".

06 — Page Sections

What stays. What goes. What changes.

Section Decision Notes for developer
Announcement Bar Keep Shorter copy. One line only. Background stays #bc535b.
Navbar Simplify 3 links max: Templates, How it works, Pricing. One CTA button.
Hero Simplify heavily Remove checklist + badges. Keep eyebrow, headline, subline, 2 buttons.
Templates Grid Keep Replace emoji placeholders with real thumbnails ASAP. 4 cards on desktop, 2 on tablet, 1 on mobile.
How It Works Keep 3 steps max. Current 3-step format is clean — don't add more.
Dark Feature Section ("Every design starts…") Remove or defer Adds no new information at this stage. Move this content into the templates section or a dedicated "About the design" page later.
Testimonials Keep 3 cards is right. Use real names and real occasions once you have them. Placeholder avatars are fine for now with initials.
Pricing Simplify layout 4 cards on desktop is crowded. Show 3 most common plans. Add a "See all" below if needed.
FAQ Keep Exactly 5–6 questions. Current questions are the right ones. Keep the accordion format.
Newsletter Remove for now Too early in the product lifecycle. Add once you have meaningful email content to send. Don't collect emails you won't use.
Footer Simplify Remove "Resources / Blog" column (no blog yet). Keep: Product, Company, Support, and a short brand blurb. 3 columns is cleaner than 5.
07 — Copy Voice

Human. Warm. Direct.

✓ Write like this
  • "A page they'll remember forever."
  • "Pick it. Fill it. Share it."
  • "No subscriptions. No surprises."
  • "Made for the moments that count."
  • "Pay once. Keep it forever."
✗ Avoid this
  • "Beautifully designed pages, ready in seconds"
  • "A whole new way to celebrate your special someone"
  • "Revolutionise how you share love"
  • "Our platform empowers you to…"
  • "Next-gen personalised gifting experience"
Rule of thumb: If your 13-year-old cousin wouldn't say it out loud, don't write it on the site. Every line should feel like something you'd text a friend.

Primary: background #1a0a0b, color #ffeff0. Secondary: transparent with border. Both use border-radius: 999px.

09 — Build Priority

Ship in this order.

#WhatWhy first
1 Colour flip + hero cleanup Biggest visual improvement for least effort. Makes the whole site feel different immediately.
2 Real template thumbnails Emoji placeholders are the single biggest trust blocker right now.
3 Remove dark feature section Cuts 30% of scroll depth. Keeps focus on what matters.
4 Simplify nav + footer Reduces cognitive load. Cleaner shell makes everything inside look better.
5 Real testimonials Replace placeholder quotes with actual customer messages — even 2–3 real ones beat 6 fake ones.
